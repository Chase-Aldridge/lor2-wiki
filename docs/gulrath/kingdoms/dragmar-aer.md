## Description

Dragmar Aer represents the economic powerhouse of Gul'Rath, where wealth determines every aspect of political and social life. The aarakocra who inhabit this kingdom have created the most powerful trader and merchant class on the continent, rivaling even military superpowers through sheer economic might and masterful spycraft.

The kingdom operates as a plutocratic matriarchy, where wealth is power and completely determines the matriarchal line of succession. This unique system ensures that the most economically successful aarakocra rise to positions of authority, creating a government deeply invested in trade, commerce, and the accumulation of resources.

---

## Government Structure

### Plutocratic Matriarchy

Dragmar Aer's government is built on the principle that wealth equals power. The matriarchal line of succession is determined entirely by economic success rather than hereditary bloodlines. The wealthiest female aarakocra becomes queen, creating an intensely competitive environment where economic acumen is the ultimate measure of fitness to rule.

**Queen Rerumaj Cloudfeather** currently holds the throne, having amassed sufficient wealth and influence to claim the position. As a Seer Sorcerer, she combines mystical insight with economic savvy, making her a formidable ruler.

### The Infinite Aerie

- **Population**: 220,000
- **Leader**: Queen Rerumaj Cloudfeather
- **Power Ranking**: B Tier
- **Alignment**: True Neutral
- **Deities**: Ket, Leviathan, Xan Yae

The Infinite Aerie is a coalition of merchant lords, master traders, and winged plutocrats under the wealthy rule of their queen. This organization is responsible for maintaining master spies, emissaries, diplomats, and traders across the whole continent. The Aerie's intelligence network rivals that of Tamaryen, though focused on economic rather than military intelligence.

## Military Power

While Dragmar Aer's B Tier ranking reflects their smaller military compared to A and S Tier powers, their military forces are highly specialized and effective:

### Elemental Alliance

The aarakocra have forged powerful alliances with djinn and elemental lords from the Plane of Fire and the Plane of Earth. These otherworldly allies provide magical support and unique tactical advantages unavailable to other kingdoms.

### Fast Attack Tactics

The Aerie's military forces are masters of fast attack tactics, utilizing:
- **Assassins** - Elite elimination specialists
- **Rogues** - Infiltration and intelligence operatives
- **Wizards** - Elemental magic users enhanced by djinn knowledge
- **Knights** - Aerial cavalry units
- **Artificers** - Creators of magical devices and weapons

The combination of natural aarakocra flight with djinn-enhanced magic creates a highly mobile strike force capable of rapid deployment and devastating hit-and-run attacks.

---

## Economic Dominance

Dragmar Aer's true power lies in its unmatched economic capabilities:

### Trade Networks

The aarakocra control the most extensive trade networks on the continent, with merchant caravans and flying messengers connecting all major kingdoms. Their aerial advantage allows them to establish trade routes impossible for ground-based civilizations.

### Spycraft and Intelligence

The kingdom's spycraft is feared across Gul'Rath. Their intelligence network, second only to Tamaryen's military espionage, focuses on economic intelligence, trade secrets, and diplomatic maneuvering. This information advantage allows them to dominate trade negotiations and predict market shifts.

### Ritual Magic

Dragmar Aer's mastery of ritual magic, particularly through their connections with elemental planes, gives them unique capabilities in:
- Weather manipulation for agricultural and trade advantage
- Planar communication with elemental lords
- Creation of powerful magical items through elemental forging
- Divination for economic forecasting

## Religion

### The Slain God Ket

Dragmar Aer primarily worshiped **Ket**, the male god of commerce and gold who appeared as an opulent cloaked lord. However, Ket has been slain (circumstances unknown), creating a religious crisis within the kingdom.

**Ket's Former Domains**: Trickery, Knowledge
**Alignment**: Chaotic Neutral
**Sacred Weapon**: Dagger
**Colors**: Orange and Blue
**Offerings**: Copper, silver, or gold coins
**Divine Plane**: The Shadow Citadel (Limbo)

### Alternative Worship

With their primary deity slain, many aarakocra have turned to:

**Leviathan** - Male god of balance who appears as a great blue dragon
**Xan Yae** - Female goddess of the unseen who appears as a slender, sincere monk

This religious diversity reflects the pragmatic True Neutral alignment of the kingdom, with different merchant lords and clans choosing deities that best serve their interests.

---

## Current Challenges

### Wild Elf Raids

Dragmar Aer has recently come under brutal raids from the remaining wild elf bloodletters pushing out from the Blood Lands. These nomadic bands target aarakocra trade caravans and settlements, disrupting commerce and threatening economic stability. The mobile nature of both aarakocra and wild elves makes this conflict particularly challenging, resembling aerial guerrilla warfare.

### Loss of Primary Deity

The death of Ket has created spiritual uncertainty within the kingdom. While the pragmatic aarakocra have adapted by adopting alternative deities, the loss of their patron god of commerce may have deeper implications for their economic power and cultural identity.

---

## Patronage and Influence

### The Explorer's Association

Dragmar Aer serves as sponsor and patron to the famed **Explorer's Association**, a massive organization of 99,999 members spanning all races. This patronage, brokered through Chief Darius, gives the kingdom:
- Access to archaeological discoveries and ancient treasures
- Intelligence from expeditions across the continent
- Influence over adventuring parties and dungeon delvers
- First access to newly discovered trade opportunities

The Explorer's Association maintains its largest headquarters in Ithiltara (aside from their office in the Aer Desert), but funding and direction flow from Dragmar Aer.

---

## Notable Locations

### Skyfall

The capital and headquarters of Queen Rerumaj Cloudfeather. This aerial city showcases aarakocra architectural genius, with buildings designed to accommodate flight and trade. Skyfall serves as the economic heart of the continent, where deals worth kingdoms are negotiated daily.

### Trade Outposts

Dragmar Aer maintains numerous trade outposts throughout Gul'Rath, each serving as an intelligence gathering center as much as a commercial hub.

## Relationships with Other Kingdoms

### Vassals

**Ithiltara** - The sea elf and gnome maritime kingdom operates as a vassal under Dragmar Aer, though the exact nature of this relationship is complex. Ithiltara is rumored to be bought and paid for, and also in debt to Dragmar Aer, making them financially dependent on aarakocra support.

### Allied Relations

- **Vol'Anlek** - Former wartime allies during The Red Crusade
- **Tamaryen** - Former wartime allies, current trading partners despite philosophical differences
- **Ironhelm** - Former wartime allies and valued trading partners for dwarven crafts

### Hostile Relations

- **The Wild Elves** - Currently conducting brutal raids against aarakocra settlements and trade routes

### Economic Partners

Dragmar Aer maintains trade relationships with virtually every kingdom and faction on the continent, leveraging their neutral stance and economic power to remain indispensable to all sides.

## The Infinite Aerie Details

The primary power structure within Dragmar Aer deserves special attention:

### Organization

The Infinite Aerie functions as both a merchant's guild and governmental body, with membership determined by wealth and trading success. The organization maintains:

- **Merchant Lords** - Wealthiest traders controlling major commercial ventures
- **Master Traders** - Experienced merchants with established networks
- **Spymasters** - Intelligence chiefs managing continental espionage
- **Emissaries** - Diplomatic representatives to foreign kingdoms
- **Artificers** - Creators of trade goods and magical items

### Intelligence Operations

The Aerie's spy network operates across all kingdoms, gathering economic intelligence, identifying trade opportunities, and monitoring potential threats to commerce. Unlike Tamaryen's military-focused espionage, Aerie agents focus on:
- Market trends and price manipulation opportunities
- Diplomatic shifts that affect trade
- Discovery of new resources or trade routes
- Identification of economic leverage against rivals

## Current Status

Dragmar Aer maintains its position as the economic powerhouse of Gul'Rath despite recent challenges. The kingdom's B Tier ranking reflects their smaller military compared to great powers, but their economic influence far exceeds their power ranking suggests.

The current era of peace has allowed Dragmar Aer to consolidate economic control through trade monopolies and vassalization of Ithiltara. However, several threats loom:

- Wild elf raids disrupting trade and threatening settlements
- The loss of patron deity Ket creating spiritual uncertainty
- Potential overextension through patronage of the Explorer's Association
- Financial control over Ithiltara creating dependency vulnerabilities
- Competition with Tamaryen for intelligence dominance

Despite these challenges, the aarakocra's mastery of trade, their alliances with elemental powers, and their continental intelligence network ensure Dragmar Aer remains one of the Great Kingdoms, proving that economic power can rival military might in shaping the fate of nations.

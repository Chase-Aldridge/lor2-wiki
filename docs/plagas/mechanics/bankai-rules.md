# Bankai Rules

## Overview

The new Bankai system for the Plagas campaign represents the ultimate expression of a character's power and connection to their abilities. This system is significantly enhanced from the first campaign, offering more flexibility, power, and strategic depth.

## Unlocking Bankai

### Requirements

- **Will be unlocked once you have a big "feel something" moment in the plot**
  - Must be a significant emotional or narrative moment
  - Connected to your character's personal journey
  - DM will work with you to identify the right moment

- **Needs to tie into your lore and/or existing class abilities somehow**
  - Should feel like a natural extension of your character
  - Reflects your character's growth and power
  - Mechanically synergistic with your build

- **Auto-Unlock at Level 13:** If you haven't "lore unlocked" Bankai by level 13, you'll naturally learn it then

---

## Activation Requirements

### Action Economy Cost
- **Uses full action AND bonus action for that turn**
- Cannot be used alongside other major actions
- Represents the intense focus required to unleash this power

### Cooldown
- **Can't use again for 1d8 days after that**
- Roll the d8 after Bankai ends to determine exact cooldown
- Represents the strain on body and spirit
- More powerful Bankai abilities may have longer cooldowns (DM discretion)

---

## Duration

### Combat Abilities
- **Only lasts 1 minute** if combat ability
- Enough time for a full combat encounter
- Must make every second count

### Utility Abilities
- **Could last up to 10 minutes** depending on DM approval
- Non-combat applications may have longer duration
- Balanced based on power level and narrative appropriateness

## Creating Your Bankai

### Design Philosophy
- **Can be an attack, ability, spell, and/or aura**
- **Near limitless options** to create what it does
- You tell the DM what you're thinking
- DM will assign the dice, rolls, checks, etc.

### Trade-Offs System
- **Will be trade-offs** built into every Bankai
- If you go pure offense, might have defense debuff
- High damage might come with self-damage or exhaustion
- Powerful utility might leave you vulnerable
- Balance is key to preventing overpowered abilities

### Example Trade-Offs
- Massive damage boost ‚Üí Reduced AC or HP
- Area control ‚Üí Limited movement
- Powerful buff ‚Üí Subsequent debuff after duration ends
- Multiple effects ‚Üí Shorter duration or longer cooldown

## Power Scaling

### Enhanced Power Level
- **Bankai is stronger now** than in the first campaign
- Can override most other actions or buffs from enemies
- Represents your character at their absolute peak

### Limitations
- **CANNOT override:**
  - Legendary enemies' abilities
  - Other Bankai
  - Domain Expansions
  - Reality-warping divine powers

## The Three Levels of Bankai

### Level 1: Base Level Bankai
**Stronger than first campaign**

- Your fundamental Bankai form
- Unlocked first (around level 13 or earlier through story)
- Already powerful but room for growth
- Core concept of your ultimate ability
- Manageable trade-offs

### Level 2: Ultimate Bankai
**More uses, more abilities, more flexibility**

- Enhanced version of base Bankai
- Unlocked through further character development
- May reduce cooldown (DM discretion)
- Additional effects or improved power
- Can add flexibility to how it's used
- Trade-offs may be reduced or new options added

### Level 3: Domain Expansion

## Bankai Examples by Concept

### Offensive Bankai
- Massive damage multipliers
- Area-of-effect devastation
- Armor penetration
- Critical hit enhancement
- Trade-off: Reduced defenses, exhaustion, or self-damage

### Defensive Bankai
- Temporary invulnerability
- Massive damage reduction
- Protective barriers for allies
- Regeneration effects
- Trade-off: Cannot attack, reduced mobility, or temporary weakness after

### Control Bankai
- Battlefield manipulation
- Enemy debuffs
- Crowd control effects
- Environmental changes
- Trade-off: Concentration required, limited duration, or positional vulnerability

### Utility Bankai
- Reality manipulation
- Time dilation
- Dimensional effects
- Information gathering
- Trade-off: Combat vulnerability, mental strain, or specific conditions required

### Hybrid Bankai
- Combination of multiple effects
- More complex mechanics
- Greater flexibility
- Trade-off: Lower peak power in any single area, or more severe drawbacks

## Strategic Considerations

### When to Use Bankai

**Best situations:**
- Boss fights or major encounters
- When the party is in serious danger
- Climactic story moments
- When victory hinges on one decisive action

**Avoid using when:**
- Minor encounters (save it!)
- Multiple tough fights expected soon (cooldown!)
- Situation can be handled with normal abilities
- Trade-offs would be crippling in current situation

### Party Coordination
- Communicate before using Bankai
- Allies can maximize your Bankai's effect
- Some Bankai may have friendly fire considerations
- Domain Expansions can affect party members (plan accordingly!)

## Design Process with DM

### Step 1: Concept
- What does your Bankai represent thematically?
- How does it connect to your character's story?
- What does it look and feel like?

### Step 2: Mechanics
- What does it actually do mechanically?
- What's the primary effect?
- Any secondary effects?

### Step 3: Balance
- DM assigns appropriate dice, DCs, and numbers
- Determine appropriate trade-offs
- Set duration and cooldown
- Test in play and adjust as needed

### Step 4: Evolution
- How will it grow into Ultimate Bankai?
- What would your Domain Expansion look like?
- Long-term character power arc

## FAQ

**Q: Can I have multiple Bankai?**
A: No, your Bankai is THE ultimate expression of your power. Quality over quantity.

**Q: Can I change my Bankai once chosen?**
A: Generally no, but character evolution might allow refinement. Discuss with DM.

**Q: What if my Bankai is too weak/strong?**
A: Balance adjustments can be made between sessions. Communication is key.

**Q: Can Bankai fail or be countered?**
A: Yes - legendary enemies, other Bankai users, or Domain Expansions can contest it.

**Q: Do I need Bankai to be effective?**
A: Absolutely not! It's a powerful tool, but smart play and teamwork matter more.
